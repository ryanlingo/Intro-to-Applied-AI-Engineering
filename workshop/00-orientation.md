# 00 · Orientation

## The one big idea

Most people use AI as an answer machine: ask, copy, forget. This workshop builds something better — a small **engine** that sits on a real project, reads your material, builds knowledge you can question, documents the work and your learning, and tells you whether to trust it.

You are not learning prompt tricks. You are learning to stay the critical thinker while an agent does the legwork.

## What you will build

A working **LLM wiki** for a project you care about, with three layers:

```text
Sources   Raw material you give it. The agent reads, never edits.
Wiki      Knowledge the agent writes and maintains for you.
Schema    AGENTS.md — the rules that keep the agent disciplined.
```

Plus two habits that run the whole time:

- **Document everything, on two tracks** — a project log and a learning log.
- **Evaluate** — a loop you run to check whether the wiki, and your own understanding, hold up.

## The four pillars

1. **Concepts** — the working vocabulary of applied AI engineering. [`01-concepts.md`](01-concepts.md)
2. **Tools** — opencode as your agent, plus an editor. [`02-setup.md`](02-setup.md)
3. **Documentation** — the wiki and the two tracks. [`03-document-and-wiki.md`](03-document-and-wiki.md)
4. **Evaluation** — trust, tested. [`04-evaluation-loop.md`](04-evaluation-loop.md)

And the throughline that ties them together: [**learning with AI**](05-learning-with-ai.md) — using the model as a tutor without letting it think for you.

## How to use this repo

- Work the modules in order. Go at your own pace; there is no clock.
- Keep [`AGENTS.md`](../AGENTS.md) open. It is the contract your agent follows, and you will edit it as you go.
- Do the hands-on steps on your *own* project material, not a toy example. The point is a wiki you keep using after the workshop.

## The mantra

> Start small. Write it down. Verify before you trust.
